Question formats

Single-correct

Pick one of four options. The classic "which of the following is correct" pattern. ≈40% of the bank.

Multi-correct

Four or five options, multiple correct. Scored all-or-nothing — partial credit isn't offered on the real exam either. ≈30% of the bank.

Negative-phrasing

"Which is incorrect / wrong / impossible." Can be single or multi. ≈30% of the bank — heavily used by the real exam.

Realistic depth

Every question cites a specific command, log path, threshold, mode name, or workflow step from the source material. No vague trivia.

How it works

Stratified sampling

Every attempt covers every module. Weights mirror module size and exam emphasis, so no chapter gets skipped.
